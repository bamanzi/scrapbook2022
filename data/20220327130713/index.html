The Star Wars franchise has spawned multiple films and television series. Two animated series were released in the mid-1980s. Further animated series began to be released in the 2000s, the first two of which focused on the prequel trilogy-era Clone Wars. After Disney's acquisition of Lucasfilm, only The Clone Wars was kept in the canon of continuity of the episodic Star Wars films. Two additional half-hour animated series were created: Rebels, which ties into the original trilogy, and Resistance, which ties into the sequel trilogy. An additional series spun off from The Clone Wars, titled The Bad Batch, debuted on Disney+ in 2021.

At least seven original Star Wars series will be released on Disney+. The first live-action series in the franchise, The Mandalorian, premiered in November 2019, and spawned three spin-offs, The Book of Boba Fett, Rangers of the New Republic and Ahsoka. Additional live-action series include Obi-Wan Kenobi, Andor, The Acolyte, and Lando.

Droids and Ewoks (1985–1987)Edit

Main articles: Star Wars: Droids and Ewoks (TV series)

Nelvana, the animation studio that produced the animated segment of the Holiday Special, was hired to create two animated series which aired together on ABC: Droids (1985–86) follows the adventures of C-3PO and R2-D2, and its sister series Ewoks (1985–86) features Wicket and other members of the titular species from Return of the Jedi, both set before the events of the original trilogy.[7][8] One reviewer calls Droids "rudimentary" and Ewoks "unremarkable", but writes that both "marked the first major effort to expand the Star Wars 'brand' beyond its original live-action film existence."[9]

The Clone Wars (2008–2020)Edit

Main article: Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)
Dave Filoni served as supervising director for both The Clone Wars and seasons 1, 2, and 4 of Rebels, before being promoted to oversee the development of all future Lucasfilm Animation projects in 2016.[10]

George Lucas created his own animation company, Lucasfilm Animation, and used it to produce his first in-house Star Wars CGI-animated series, using Cartoon Network's 2003 Clone Wars micro-series as a "pilot".[11] The Clone Wars (2008–2020) was introduced through a 2008 animated film of the same name.[12] The series is set between Attack of the Clones and Revenge of the Sith of the prequel trilogy.[13] It focuses mainly on the Jedi characters of Anakin Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi, as well as Anakin's Padawan apprentice, Ahsoka Tano (an original character created by Filoni for the series), with other characters from the live-action films in supporting roles. The series marked the beginning of Dave Filoni's involvement in Star Wars animation projects.

After Disney's acquisition of the Star Wars franchise, The Clone Wars was cancelled in 2014 before its intended final episodes were completed. The remaining unaired episodes were released on Netflix as "The Lost Missions". The chronological storyline order was released after the series had initially finished airing.[14] The film and series were included in the canon established in 2014.[15][16] The show was later revived for an additional final season that premiered on February 21, 2020, on the streaming service Disney+.[17][18]

Rebels (2014–2018)Edit

Main article: Star Wars Rebels

In 2014, Disney XD began airing Star Wars Rebels, the first CGI-animated series produced following the Disney acquisition. It follows a band of rebels as they fight the Galactic Empire in the years leading up to A New Hope.[13] It closed some of the arcs introduced in The Clone Wars.[19][20][21] Due to the film Rogue One being produced at the same time, the film and the series acknowledged each other.[22][23] The series also included an in-canon version of Grand Admiral Thrawn from the Legends Thrawn trilogy.[24]

Resistance (2018–2020)Edit

Main article: Star Wars Resistance

The animated series Star Wars Resistance debuted in late 2018, shifting the animation style towards anime-inspired cel-shading visuals, and focuses on a young Resistance pilot Kazuda Xiono before and during The Force Awakens and The Last Jedi, leading up to The Rise of Skywalker.[25][26] Sequel trilogy characters such as Captain Phasma, General Hux, and Kylo Ren appear. The second and final season premiered on October 6, 2019.[27]

The Bad Batch (2021–present)Edit

Main article: Star Wars: The Bad Batch

Set after the events of The Clone Wars, The Bad Batch follows the titular squad making their way through the galaxy as mercenaries in the early days of the Empire. The series was announced in July 2020, and premiered on Disney+ on May 4, 2021.[28] Dave Filoni, Athena Portillo, Brad Rau, and Jennifer Corbett serve as executive producers, with Carrie Beck as co-executive producer and Josh Rimes as producer. Rau also serves as supervising director with Corbett the head writer.[4]

While the first season was still airing, Lucasfilm announced that the series would return for a second season in 2022.[29]

Visions (2021)Edit

Main article: Star Wars: Visions

Announced on December 10, 2020, Star Wars: Visions is an anthology anime series of nine short films by different creators, set in the Star Wars universe but not restricted to the canonical timeline. It released on Disney+ on September 22, 2021.[30][31] Footage from the project was first revealed at Anime Expo on July 3. The anthology featured short films produced by Kamikaze Douga, Geno Studio, Studio Colorido, Studio Trigger, Kinema Citrus, Science SARU, and Production I.G.[31]

Clone Wars (2003–2005)Edit

Main article: Star Wars: Clone Wars (2003 TV series)

After the release of Attack of the Clones, developed and drawn by Genndy Tartakovsky. Cartoon Network produced and aired the micro-series Clone Wars from 2003 to weeks before the 2005 release of Revenge of the Sith, as the series featured events set between those films.[41][42]

The plot in the series was intended as prequel to then upcoming Revenge of The Sith and directly leads to the starting point in the movie.

It won the Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Animated Program in 2004 and 2005.[43][44]

Blips (2017)Edit

Star Wars Blips is an animated micro-series created to promote The Last Jedi. It features droids such as BB-8 and R2-D2, in addition to porgs.[45]

Forces of Destiny (2017–2018)Edit

Main article: Star Wars Forces of Destiny

The animated micro-series Star Wars Forces of Destiny debuted in 2017; it focuses on the female characters of the franchise and is set in various eras.[46]

Galaxy of Adventures (2018–2020)Edit

Star Wars Galaxy of Adventures debuted on the "Star Wars Kids" YouTube channel and website in late 2018. Using stylized animation, the series of shorts reimagine key scenes from the saga,[47] initially leading up the release of The Rise of Skywalker.[48][49] The shorts feature audio from the original films (with narration by Dante Basco) and are animated by Titmouse, Inc.[48][50]

Roll Out (2019–2020)Edit

Star Wars Roll Out debuted on the Star Wars Kids YouTube channel and website in August 2019. The shorts depict the main characters from the franchise as ball-shaped, similar to BB-8, and utilize a kid-friendly cutout animation style.[39] io9 calls the design of the series "adorable".[51]

Galaxy of Creatures (2021)Edit

Star Wars Galaxy of Creatures debuted on the Star Wars Kids YouTube channel and website in October 2021. The shorts depict the droid SF-R3 ("Aree") as he journeys across the galaxy learning about various wildlife.[40]

The Mandalorian (2019–present)Edit

Main article: The Mandalorian

In March 2018, Jon Favreau was hired to write and produce a live-action Star Wars series for Disney+.[69] The series follows "a lone gunfighter in the outer reaches of the galaxy far from the authority of the New Republic"[70] a few years after the events of Return of the Jedi.[71] In August 2018, it was reported that the series would cost about "$100 million for 10 episodes".[72] Pedro Pascal stars as the titular character,[73] and is joined by supporting actors Gina Carano, Nick Nolte, Giancarlo Esposito, Emily Swallow, Carl Weathers, Omid Abtahi, and Werner Herzog.[74] The series premiered on November 12, 2019, with the launch of Disney+.[75] The second season premiered on October 30, 2020,[76] while the third season is expected to premiere in 2022.[54]

The Book of Boba Fett (2021–present)Edit

Main article: The Book of Boba Fett

In November 2020, Deadline Hollywood reported that a miniseries focused on Boba Fett could begin filming before the end of the year, before a third season of The Mandalorian went into production.[77] The series, known as The Book of Boba Fett, was revealed the following month, and confirmed to be in production ahead of The Mandalorian season three. Favreau and Filoni executive produce along with Robert Rodriguez, with Temuera Morrison and Ming-Na Wen reprising their roles as Boba Fett and Fennec Shand, respectively.[53] It premiered on December 29, 2021, and consisted of seven episodes.[78][79]

Obi-Wan KenobiEdit

Main article: Obi-Wan Kenobi (TV series)

In August 2019, it was reported that a series focused on Obi-Wan Kenobi was in development, with Ewan McGregor in talks to reprise his role from the prequel trilogy. The series was initially thought to have been developing as a standalone film, which was ultimately scrapped due to the performance of Solo at the box office.[80] During the 2019 D23 Expo, Lucasfilm officially announced that the series was in development, with McGregor confirmed to reprise his role as the titular character.[81] It will take place eight years after Revenge of the Sith, thus exploring the time during Kenobi's exile on Tatooine prior to the events of A New Hope.[82] In September 2019, Deborah Chow and Hossein Amini were announced as the series' director and writer, respectively, who will also serve as executive producers alongside McGregor.[58] In April 2020, it was announced that Joby Harold would officially be taking over writing duties from Amini.[83] In August 2020, Kennedy told TheWrap that it would be a limited series.[84] Filming had begun by May 2021.[85]

On Disney Investor Day 2020, it was revealed that Hayden Christensen would reprise his role as Darth Vader, and that the series would be titled Obi-Wan Kenobi.[67] It is scheduled to premiere on May 25, 2022,[57] and will consist of six episodes.[56]

AndorEdit

Main article: Andor (TV series)

The series will follow Rebel spy Cassian Andor, five years before the events of Rogue One, during the formative years of the Rebellion.[86][87]

In November 2018, a prequel series to the 2016 film Rogue One centered on Cassian Andor was confirmed to be in development, and described as a "rousing spy thriller". Diego Luna will reprise his role for the series.[86] Stephen Schiff was hired to serve as showrunner and executive producer of the series, which was developed by Jared Bush, who wrote a series bible and a draft of the pilot episode.[88] In April 2019, Alan Tudyk was confirmed to be reprising his role of K-2SO.[89] In April 2020, Tony Gilroy, who worked on the reshoots of Rogue One, replaced Schiff as showrunner. Gilroy was going to write and direct the pilot, and direct other episodes as well.[63] It was also revealed in April 2020 that Stellan Skarsgård, Kyle Soller, Denise Gough had joined the cast in undisclosed roles, along with Genevieve O'Reilly reprising her role of Mon Mothma.[87][90] In September 2020, it was announced that Gilroy would step down from his directing duties in light of the COVID-19 pandemic and would be replaced by Toby Haynes, who is set to direct the first three episodes.[91] Filming began in late November 2020 in London.[92] Andor is expected to be released in mid-to-late 2022,[62] and will consist of 12 episodes.[61] A second season is in development.[64]

AhsokaEdit

See also: The Mandalorian § Ahsoka

In December 2020, Ahsoka was announced. Developed by Favreau and Filoni,[65] it exists alongside The Mandalorian, The Book of Boba Fett and Rangers of the New Republic through interconnected stories culminating in a "climactic story event".[65][93] Rosario Dawson headlines the limited series, reprising her role as Ahsoka Tano.[93] In October 2021, The Hollywood Reporter announced that Hayden Christensen would reprise his role as Anakin Skywalker in the series, which is set to begin filming in early 2022.[94] In November, Deadline Hollywood announced that Natasha Liu Bordizzo was cast as Sabine Wren, a character first introduced in Star Wars Rebels.[95] In January 2022, The Hollywood Reporter announced that Mary Elizabeth Winstead is cast in an undisclosed role.[96] The next month, The Hollywood Reporter announced that Ray Stevenson was cast in an undisclosed role.[97]

The AcolyteEdit

See also: Star Wars: The High Republic § History

In April 2020, Variety reported that a female-centric live-action Star Wars series is in development for Disney+ with Russian Doll co-creator Leslye Headland serving as a writer and showrunner.[98] In early November, Headland expounded that the series would be set "in a pocket of the universe and a pocket of the timeline that we don't know much about," elaborating that she was more engaged creatively with the geography of the Star Wars universe than its existing visuals.[99] On November 5, Deadline reported that the series was expected to be an "action thriller with martial arts elements".[100] On Disney Investor Day 2020, it was revealed that it would take place during the late High Republic era.[67] Filming is expected to begin in mid-2022 in London, with the series consisting of eight episodes.[66]

LandoEdit

See also: Lando Calrissian § Television series

In December 2020, Lando was announced as a Disney+ limited series, focusing on the titular character, with a story by Justin Simien.[67]

Rangers of the New RepublicEdit

See also: The Mandalorian § Spin-offs

In December 2020, Rangers of the New Republic was announced. Developed by Favreau and Filoni,[65] it was planned to tie into The Mandalorian, The Book of Boba Fett and Ahsoka.[65][93] Variety reported in May 2021 that the series was not in active development.[68] The series was still considered "on hold" in November 2021, Kennedy saying development had not reached a point where scripts were written, and that she felt some of the ideas for the series would "figure into future episodes ... of the next iteration of Mandalorian".[101]

Untitled seriesEdit

In February 2022, Production Weekly revealed the existence of an upcoming, untitled Star Wars series that was using the working title Grammar Rodeo.[102] It was later reported that Jon Watts was being considered to direct at least one episode of the series, along with Favreau as an executive producer. The series was reported to be set during the High Republic era, with casting set to include four teenage actors and one 30-to-40-year old actor as its series regulars. Filming was set to being as early as June 2022 and last until December 2022 at Manhattan Beach Studios, with a formal announcement planned for the series at Star Wars celebration in May 2022.[103][104]

Jedi Temple Challenge (2020)Edit

Star Wars: Jedi Temple Challenge is a web-based children's game show developed by Lucasfilm. First announced in December 2019,[105][106] the series debuted on the Star Wars Kids website and YouTube channel on June 10, 2020.[107] The series features young contestants competing as Jedi Padawans in a series of obstacle courses and knowledge tests in the attempt to gain the rank of Jedi Knight. The show is hosted by Ahmed Best, who plays the character of Kelleran Beq, the Jedi Master mentor of the contestants who is joined by his droid companions, AD-3 (voiced by Mary Holland and performed by Gordon Tarpley) and LX-R5. Sam Witwer provides the voice of an unknown character of the Dark Side of the Force during the final challenge of each episode.[108]

Abandoned projectsEdit

UnderworldEdit

In 2005, plans for a live-action television series set between the prequel and original trilogies were announced at Star Wars Celebration.[155] In 2007, Lucas described the project as "one show that will split into four shows, focusing on different characters."[156] It entered development in early 2009.[157] The series was described as "gritty and dark" and was expected to feature characters such as Han Solo, Chewbacca, Lando Calrissian,[158] Boba Fett,[159] C-3PO, and Emperor Palpatine. Lucas described the series as "more talky. It's more of what I would call a soap opera with a bunch of personal dramas in it. It's not really based on action-adventure films from the '30s—it's actually more based on film noir movies from the '40s!"[160] Producer Rick McCallum revealed the working title, Star Wars: Underworld, in 2012,[161] and that it would focus on criminal and political power struggles in the "period when the Empire is trying to take things over."[162]

Over one hundred 42-minute episodes were planned,[156] with 50 scripts written.[163] These were mostly second drafts, but due to their complex content, were too expensive to produce.[157] Ronald D. Moore was one of the writers, and extensive artwork including character, costume, and set designs were developed at Skywalker Ranch under the close supervision of Lucas and McCallum.[164] The project was still being considered after Lucasfilm was sold to Disney, including by ABC,[164] with stories being reviewed as of December 2015[update].[165] According to Kathleen Kennedy,

That's an area we've spent a lot of time, reading through the material that he developed is something we very much would like to explore. ... So our attitude is, we don't want to throw any of that stuff away. It's gold. And it's something we're spending a lot of time looking at, pouring [sic] through, discussing, and we may very well develop those things further. We definitely want to.[155]

The plot of the anthology film Rogue One: A Star Wars Story was originally pitched as an episode of the series.[166] The story of Han winning the Millennium Falcon from Lando was planned to be featured[158] and later developed as Solo: A Star Wars Story. Boba Fett was also reported to appear in the show, Star Wars author, Karen Traviss, was to write a novel involving Boba Fett, but the project was reportedly canceled because of possible conflicts with the TV series.[167] In early 2020, Stargate Studios shared test footage made to promote the series to networks, as well as a Google Doc listing production details for the series.[168][169]

DetoursEdit

Main article: Star Wars Detours

Star Wars Detours is an unaired animated parody series from the creators of Robot Chicken, which was postponed in 2013 and ultimately unaired.[170] Production began in 2012 prior to the Disney acquisition,[171] with 39 episodes completed and 62 additional scripts finished.[172]

Cara Dune-centered seriesEdit

A Mandalorian spin-off series centered on the character Cara Dune was reportedly considered for Disney+ by November 2020, which Lucasfilm had intended to announce at Disney's Investor Day event the next month.[173][174] However, after Gina Carano, who portrayed Dune in The Mandalorian, posted controversial statements during November 2020 and February 2021, any such project was cancelled and Disney severed ties with the actress.[173]
